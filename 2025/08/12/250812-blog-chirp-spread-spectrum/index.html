Paper 23 - MIMO-Based Chirp Spread Spectrum With Permutation Matrix Modulation

Channel and Signal Model

PMM Scheme

Assume Np = ⌊log2min (Nt!, Nr!)⌋. For a normal case of Nr ≥ Nt, we have Np = ⌊log2Nt!⌋ bits for symbol mapping. Specifically, for the transmitted symbols xt = [x0, …, xNt − 1], the corresponding transmitted symbols from antennas are given by xt, P = Pxt where P ∈ 𝒫 = {P0, …, P2Np}.

MIMO Scheme

The MIMO channel is constructed by a channel matrix H ∈ ℂNr × Nt. With the allocated power matrix of Γ = diag{γ1, …, γNt}, the received symbols can be written as Y = HPΓX + W where X ∈ ℂNt × M denotes the corresponding chirps within each antenna and each entry in W follows 𝒞N(0, σ2).

It has been proven in [23] that γ1 ≠ … ≠ γNt must hold.

Semi-coherent Detection

The transmitter sends a consecutive sequence of up-chirps and down-chirps to achieve channel estimation (without synchronization). The channel estimation renders Ns steps, where each step utilizes the successive transmitted up-chirps from every individual antenna. Then, the columns of corresponding channel matrix can be estimated afterwards by LS. Averaging the estimated results within Ns steps yields the estimated channel matrix. The detection for P and C follows.

The MSE of the estimates with respect to the effective channel matrix is also given.
