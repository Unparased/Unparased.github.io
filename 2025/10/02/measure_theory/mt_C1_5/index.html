Chapter 1.5 Completeness and Regularity

DEF - The measure μ (or the measure space (X, 𝒜, μ) is complete if the relations A ∈ 𝒜, μ(A) = 0, and B ⊆ A together imply that B ∈ 𝒜. [Completeness iff all negligible subsets belong to A]

DEF - A subset B of X is μ-negligible (or μ-null) if there is a subset A of X such that A ∈ 𝒜, B ∈ A, and μ(A) = 0.

  • If μ* is an outer measure on X and if ℳμ* is the σ-algebra of all μ*-measurable subsets of X, then the restriction of μ* to ℳμ* is complete.
    • So is the Lebesgue measure on Lebesgue measurable set.
    • The restriction of Lebesgue measure to the σ-algebra of Borel subsets of ℝd is not complete. [*]

DEF [Completion] - Define a function μ̄ : 𝒜μ → [0, ∞] by letting μ̄(A) be the common value of μ(E) and μ(F), where E, F ∈ 𝒜 and E ⊆ A ⊆ F, μ(F − E) = 0. Then, μ̄ is the completion of μ.

  • The completion of 𝒜 under μ is the collection 𝒜μ of subsets A of X for which there are sets E and F in 𝒜 such that E ⊆ A ⊆ F and μ(F − E) = 0.

Proposition 1.5.1 - Let (X, 𝒜) be a measurable space, and let μ be a measure on 𝒜. Then 𝒜μ is a σ-algebra on X that includes 𝒜, and μ̄ is a measure on 𝒜μ that is complete and whose restriction to 𝒜 is μ.

Proposition 1.5.2 - Lebesgue measure on (ℝd, ℳλ*) is the completion of Lebesgue measure on (ℝd, ℬ(ℝd)).

Lemma 1.5.3 - Let A be a Lebesgue measurable subset of ℝd. Then there exist Borel subsets E and F of ℝd such that E ⊆ A ⊆ F and λ(F − E) = 0. [To prove Proposition 1.5.2]

Sec. 2.1 will introduce:

  • There are Lebesgue measurable subsets of ℝ that are not Borel sets.
  • The restriction of Lebesgue measure to ℬ(ℝ) is not complete.

DEF [Outer measure] - The outer measure of A, μ*(A), an arbitrary subset of X, is defined by μ*(A) = inf {μ(B) : A ⊆ B and B ∈ 𝒜}. DEF [Inner measure] - The inner measure of A, μ*(A), an arbitrary subset of X, is defined by μ*(A) = sup {μ(B) : B ⊆ A and B ∈ 𝒜}. Proposition 1.5.4 - Let (X, 𝒜, μ) be a measure space. Then the function μ* : 𝒫(X) → [0, ∞] defined by Outer measure is an outer measure on X.

Proposition 1.5.5 - Let (X, 𝒜, μ) be a measure space. Let A be a subset of X such that μ*(A) < ∞. Then A belongs to 𝒜μ iff μ*(A) = μ*(A).

DEF [Regularity] - Let 𝒜 be a σ-algebra on ℝd that includes the σ-algebra ℬ(ℝd) of Borel sets. A measure μ on (ℝd, 𝒜) is regular if

  • each compact subset K of ℝd satisfies μ(K) < ∞,

  • each set A in 𝒜 satisfies μ(A) = inf {μ(U) : A ⊆ U, U is open},

  • each open subset U in 𝒜 satisfies μ(U) = sup {μ(K) : K ⊆ U, K is compact},
